About Digscord

Digscord is a collaborative platform that unites civilians and archeologists to ethically document artifacts, identify potential sites, and support community-backed excavations.

Mission

Respect the land, honor the past, and empower communities to participate in responsible archeology.

Approach

Combine AI-assisted identification with crowdsourced observations and expert review.

Ethics

Center indigenous perspectives, uphold legal standards, and protect sensitive sites.

Frequently asked questions

Is this a real identification AI?

This demo simulates AI results. A production system would require trained models and expert validation.

Do I need permission to log artifacts?

Logging observations is generally allowed, but collecting artifacts may be illegal on private or public lands without permits.

How are sensitive locations handled?

Sensitive coordinates should be protected. Public sharing is limited to generalized regions.

Can archeologists request more info?

Yes. Chat rooms connect archeologists and civilians to gather context before site visits.

How are funds managed?

Archeologists control campaigns and must meet legal and ethical standards for excavation.

Legal & ethical

Core guidance

  • Non-removal: Leave artifacts where found unless explicitly permitted.
  • Reporting: Notify authorities when appropriate.
  • Sovereignty: Respect tribal lands and community leadership.

Scenario narrative

A hiker stumbles upon an arrowhead glinting in soft light after a rainstorm. Curious but cautious, they open Digscord, snap a clear photo with scale, and within moments the app suggests a likely classification. A warning banner reminds them: leave it in place, document context, respect laws. The artifact joins their virtual collection, anchoring a memory to a geographically grounded observation. Days later, a verified archeology student narrows filters to the same type and time range. The Digscord map reveals a neat cluster around the DRSS address. The student notifies contributors, opens a chat, and begins gathering details about soil strata and distances to waterways. A community fund springs up, a volunteer roster grows, and with initial survey notes crowd-sourced, a university grant is approved. The dig commencesâ€”with clear permits, respect for tribal sovereignty, and the local community invited to witness the unfolding story beneath their feet.

Photography checklist

  • Scale: Include a ruler or coin.
  • Angles: Capture top, side, and edge views.
  • Lighting: Avoid harsh shadows; diffuse if possible.

Safety practices

  • Permits: Verify permissions before any work.
  • Gear: Wear gloves and protective eyewear when needed.
  • Boundaries: Respect closed areas and sensitive sites.

Community engagement ideas

Host weekend walk-throughs, share findings responsibly, and invite local schools for educational visits.

Grant writing tips

  • Evidence: Include crowdsourced survey and timestamps.
  • Budget: Clarify line items and community support.
  • Impact: Emphasize public education and preservation.

Post-dig reporting

Publish summaries, anonymize sensitive data, and share educational materials with the community.
